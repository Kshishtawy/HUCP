As previously discussed, Computer Science is not a hardware-based study, in fact, it is largely based on mathematics and mathematical equations.
The operation of computers started only after advances in mathematics and logic. The mathematical foundation of computers is logic.
Other fields like calculus, probability theory, and set theory are mathematical fields that are applied in computers programs but are not very important. However, logic can be a basis for all types of mathematics.

Who other to talk about when mathematics is mentioned other than the father of algebra, Muhammed al-Khwarizmi.
Having been the first known mathematician to work on algorism in 830 BC.
which is the technique used to perform basic arithmetic using Hindu-Arabic numerals. Algorithm was then derived from the study of algorithm.

Then in 1815 came the largely self-taught mathematician, George Boole, who invented Boolean algebra. It is a system where there are only 2 possible values, either 1 or 0, which has greatly contributed in the invention of logic gates later on in 1954.

In 1815 was also Ada Lovelace,
which was considered the first ever computer scientist for writing about her (The Analytical Engine.)
She worked alongside Charles Baggage, also an English mathematician,
whose work she became interested in as early as 1833.
She programed for her Analytical Engine, where the early programming language (Ada) was named after her.
As Baggage worked on building it, sadly he was never able to completely built the engine.

In 1912 was Alan Turing, previously mentioned, was both a computer scientist and a mathematician. Mathematically he worked on computation theory, for which he then designed his turing machine - an entirely virtual abstract computing device able to perform mathematical equations and follow instructions. Using his machine, Turing was able to place a fundamental limit on the power of computation as he figured some mathematical equations that could not be solved using algorithms. Turing is considered a founding father of artifical intelligence and theoretical computer science.
